FEDERAL HIGHWAY ADMINISTRATION
HYDRAULIC ENGINEERING CIRCULAR — ROADWAY DRAINAGE DESIGN
CHAPTER 4 — STORM DRAIN SYSTEMS (SAMPLE EXCERPT FOR DEMO PURPOSES)

4.1 DESIGN STORM FREQUENCY
Storm drain systems for interstate and freeway facilities shall be designed to
convey, at a minimum, the peak flow from a 10-year design storm without surcharge.
Cross culverts and structures within the floodplain shall be evaluated for the
100-year event to confirm no increase in upstream flood elevation.

4.2 PIPE MATERIALS AND MINIMUM COVER
Reinforced concrete pipe (RCP) is the preferred material beneath roadway
pavement sections carrying interstate traffic. Minimum cover over the crown of
the pipe shall be 1 foot for pipe diameters up to 36 inches, and 2 feet for
larger diameters, measured from the top of pipe to the bottom of pavement
structure. Corrugated metal pipe (CMP) may be permitted for side-drain
applications outside the roadway prism, subject to Department approval.

4.3 INLET SPACING AND CAPACITY
Curb inlets on grade shall be spaced such that gutter spread does not exceed
one-half of the travel lane width during the design storm. Sag inlets shall be
sized to pass the design flow with a maximum ponding depth of 0.5 feet above
the gutter flow line, plus a debris/clogging factor of 50 percent applied to
the theoretical inlet capacity.

4.4 UTILITY CONFLICTS
Where proposed storm drain alignments conflict with existing utilities, the
Contractor shall notify the Engineer and submit a Request for Information
(RFI) identifying the conflict, proposed resolution, and any associated cost
or schedule impact prior to proceeding with excavation in the conflict area.

4.5 CONSTRUCTION SEQUENCING AND MAINTENANCE OF TRAFFIC
Storm drain installation beneath active travel lanes shall be sequenced to
minimize the duration of lane closures and shall conform to the approved
Maintenance of Traffic (MOT) plan. Temporary drainage measures, including
diversion ditches or temporary pipe, shall be provided to prevent ponding on
travel lanes during construction.

4.6 INSPECTION AND ACCEPTANCE
Prior to backfill, all storm drain pipe joints and structures shall be
inspected for proper bedding, alignment, and joint sealing. Deficiencies shall
be corrected before backfilling. Post-construction CCTV inspection is required
for all mainline storm drain pipe 24 inches in diameter or greater.
