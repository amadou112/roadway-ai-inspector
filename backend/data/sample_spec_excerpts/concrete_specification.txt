DELAWARE DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION
STANDARD SPECIFICATIONS FOR ROAD AND BRIDGE CONSTRUCTION
SECTION 812 — PORTLAND CEMENT CONCRETE (SAMPLE EXCERPT FOR DEMO PURPOSES)

812.01 DESCRIPTION
This work consists of furnishing and placing Portland cement concrete for bridge
decks, substructures, and roadway structures in accordance with these
specifications and the dimensions shown on the plans.

812.02 MATERIALS
(a) Cement shall conform to AASHTO M 85, Type II, unless otherwise specified.
(b) Coarse and fine aggregates shall conform to Section 703.
(c) Water shall be free of oil, acid, alkali, organic matter, or other
    deleterious substances.
(d) Air-entraining admixtures shall conform to AASHTO M 154.

812.03 CONCRETE MIX DESIGN
Class A concrete (bridge decks) shall attain a minimum 28-day compressive
strength of 4,500 psi. Class B concrete (substructures, footings) shall attain
a minimum 28-day compressive strength of 3,500 psi. The Contractor shall
submit a mix design for each class of concrete for the Engineer's approval no
fewer than 21 calendar days prior to the first placement.

812.04 CURING
All exposed concrete surfaces shall be cured for a minimum of 7 calendar days
using one of the following methods: (1) continuous water curing, (2) curing
compound conforming to AASHTO M 148 applied within 30 minutes of finishing, or
(3) impervious sheeting. Curing compound shall not be used on surfaces that
will later receive a bonded overlay or waterproofing membrane.

812.05 COLD WEATHER PLACEMENT
Concrete shall not be placed when the ambient temperature is below 40°F unless
the Contractor submits a cold-weather concreting plan for the Engineer's
approval, including provisions for maintaining the concrete temperature above
50°F for a minimum of 72 hours after placement.

812.06 REINFORCING STEEL COVER
Minimum clear cover over reinforcing steel shall be 2.5 inches for bridge deck
top mat exposed to deicing chemicals, and 1.5 inches for substructure elements
not exposed to deicing chemicals, unless otherwise noted on the plans.
Deficient cover identified during inspection shall be documented on a
Non-Conformance Report and corrected prior to placement of concrete.

812.07 ACCEPTANCE TESTING
Compressive strength shall be verified by test cylinders cast in accordance
with AASHTO T 23, one set per 50 cubic yards placed or per placement, whichever
is more frequent. Any test result below 90 percent of the specified strength
shall be reported immediately to the Resident Engineer.
